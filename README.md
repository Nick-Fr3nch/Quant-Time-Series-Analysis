AAPL Options Volatility Analysis
This project analyzes implied volatility dynamics and term structure inversions for AAPL options.

Deliverables
Analysis: analysis.ipynb

Report: Report.pdf

Slide: Slide.pdf

Data: options_data.csv

References:
Financial Theory & Quantitative Methodology
Black, F., & Scholes, M. (1973). "The Pricing of Options and Corporate Liabilities." Journal of Political Economy, 81(3), 637-654. (The foundational model for the implied volatility calculations analyzed in this project).

Hull, J. C. (2017). Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives (10th ed.). Pearson. (Used for the theoretical framework of Volatility Term Structures and Mean Reversion).

Natenberg, S. (1994). Option Volatility and Pricing: Advanced Trading Strategies and Techniques. McGraw-Hill. (Referenced for interpreting the Volatility Skew and market risk sentiment).

Whaley, R. E. (2000). "The Investor Fear Gauge." The Journal of Portfolio Management, 26(3), 12-17. (Context for the "Fear Regime" analysis identified in the AAPL near-term IV spike).

Data Sources & Technical Documentation
Arakaki, R., et al. (2024). yfinance User Guide. Open-source documentation for the Yahoo Finance API interface.

Harris, C. R., et al. (2020). "Array programming with NumPy." Nature, 585, 357–362.

McKinney, W. (2010). "Data Structures for Statistical Computing in Python." Proceedings of the 9th Python in Science Conference. (Documentation for the Pandas library used for time-series manipulation).

Waskom, M. L. (2021). "Seaborn: statistical data visualization." Journal of Open Source Software, 6(60), 3021. (Used for generating the Volatility Surface and Term Structure visualizations).
